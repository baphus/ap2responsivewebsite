The Joy in Performing

April 2, 2025 Music

Last year’s intramurals at Cebu Technological University was more than just the usual sports events and cheering squads — for me, it was about finally getting to perform live as a guitarist. And let me tell you… it was awesome.

There’s something magical about being on stage, guitar in hand, music echoing through the crowd. It wasn’t just about playing notes — it was about the energy, the crowd reactions, and the connection with the people I was playing with. We weren’t just students anymore; we were performers, and for that moment, it felt like the whole campus was vibing with us.

Meeting the Band, Catching the Vibe

One of the highlights? Meeting our drummer. Total stranger at first, but after a few jam sessions, we just clicked. It’s rare to find someone you can musically sync with so naturally, and jamming with them made every practice session something I actually looked forward to.

We all got a say in what songs to play, and I had a blast suggesting tracks that would get the crowd hyped. Practicing together, refining the flow, tightening transitions — it wasn’t always perfect, but it was always fun.

The Performance

When we finally got on stage during the intrams, all the nerves melted away. The adrenaline, the cheers, the rhythm — it was pure joy. Seeing people react, sing along, and enjoy the set made every practice hour worth it.

Honestly, it feels like the browser that’s quietly doing everything right without screaming for attention.

Already Looking Forward to the Next One

That experience reminded me of why I love music in the first place. It connects people. It creates moments. And it makes you feel alive. I honestly hope I get the chance to do it again — maybe even with the same group (shoutout to our drummer!).

Until then, I’ll keep jamming, keep practicing, and keep waiting for that next opportunity to share the stage and the music again.
